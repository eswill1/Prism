# Prism Front Page Benchmark
### Version 0.2 — Updated 2026-03-12: three-column newsroom homepage direction adopted

---

## Outlets Reviewed

This benchmark is based on a March 11, 2026 review of ten mainstream U.S.-focused news products and adjacent large national digital outlets:

1. The Washington Post
2. Associated Press
3. ABC News
4. CBS News
5. Fox News
6. NBC News
7. CNN
8. NPR
9. USA TODAY
10. Axios

BBC was also used as a non-U.S. reference point for homepage behavior, but it is not counted in the ten.

---

## Shared Front Page Patterns

Across these outlets, the strongest common patterns are:

- the homepage is the actual news destination
- one lead story package dominates the top of the page
- several supporting headlines sit nearby
- a right rail or adjacent column often carries fresher, faster-moving items
- broad categories help readers scan quickly
- freshness is visible through timestamps, live tags, or update signals
- the page is overwhelmingly news content, not product explanation

What does **not** dominate these front pages:

- methodology explainers
- product onboarding panels
- business-model messaging
- feature marketing

Those elements exist, but they sit outside the main news-reading path.

## Outlet Notes

### The Washington Post

- broad top navigation includes Politics, Climate, Business, Tech, World, local coverage, and Sports
- clear lead story package followed by exclusives, analysis, and more top stories
- homepage is dense with news and visibly updated throughout the day

Source:
- https://www.washingtonpost.com/

### Associated Press

- section-led homepage with World, U.S., Politics, Business, Tech, Climate, Health, and more
- each section has top stories immediately visible on the homepage
- very strong news-first density with minimal product clutter

Source:
- https://apnews.com/

### ABC News

- homepage mixes top stories, live coverage, and section tags such as Politics, U.S., Business, Lifestyle, and Health
- repeated use of section labels reinforces scanning behavior
- live coverage exists, but the homepage still acts as the main front door

Source:
- https://abcnews.com/

### CBS News

- homepage explicitly breaks into Politics and World sections, among others
- clean section-based scroll behavior
- homepage remains focused on headlines and packages, not self-description

Source:
- https://www.cbsnews.com/

### Fox News

- very strong section navigation with U.S., Politics, World, Opinion, Entertainment, and many subtopics
- homepage is headline-heavy and updates quickly
- large amount of content, but still clearly organized by news categories

Source:
- https://www.foxnews.com/

### NBC News

- official product description emphasizes top stories in world news, business, politics, health, and pop culture
- app positioning emphasizes top stories, breaking news, live coverage, and deeper topic following

Sources:
- https://www.nbcnews.com/information/nbc-news-info/contact-us-n1232521
- https://apps.nbcnews.com/mobile/

### CNN

- official fact sheet describes CNN Digital programming across news, sports, business, politics, style, entertainment, climate, weather, science and technology, travel, space, health, food, nature, and special sections
- digital positioning is immediate, multi-format, and section-rich

Source:
- https://cnnpressroom.blogs.cnn.com/cnn-fact-sheet/

### NPR

- homepage and network positioning emphasize a local-national-global blend
- topic surfaces include politics, health, immigration, education, technology, and culture-adjacent coverage
- strong sense of editorial identity, but the front page remains story-led

Sources:
- https://www.npr.org/
- https://www.npr.org/network/
- https://www.npr.org/newsletters/

### USA TODAY

- official positioning emphasizes being quick to read, useful, and not information overload
- subscription/help materials explicitly refer to the homepage and section fronts as the accessible news surface
- national flagship identity is reinforced by local-to-national network coverage

Sources:
- https://pantheon-marketing.usatoday.com/about-us/
- https://help.usatoday.com/

### Axios

- homepage uses a lead list plus major topic nav: Politics & Policy, Business, Technology, Health, Energy & Climate
- content is highly scannable and concise
- explanatory product language is minimal compared with the amount of news on screen

Source:
- https://www.axios.com/

## What Prism Should Copy

- one obvious lead package
- a nearby stack of secondary headlines
- broad familiar categories
- timestamps and update signals
- front page density that says "news" immediately

## What Prism Should Not Copy Blindly

- cluttered legacy-newspaper sprawl
- generic infinite-feed behavior
- loud breaking-news theatrics
- opinion or punditry dominating the top of the page

---

## Prism Front Page Rules

The Prism homepage should:

- be almost entirely news content
- keep explanation and trust pages off the main reading path
- open every story card into the Prism story page
- use section labels for wayfinding, not for replacing story-first structure
- use a three-column top deck with a lead package, supporting story blocks, and a latest-news right rail
